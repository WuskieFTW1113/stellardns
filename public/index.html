⇄ LANCache rewrites & launcher toggles

Each category answers with your cache IP for every domain it owns — a consistent static rewrite, no lancache-dns container. Toggle a launcher off to return NXDOMAIN.

⌗ Domain editor

Pick a category to see its domains. Add, remove, or move a domain to another category — rewrites are fully interchangeable.

⊘ Blocklists

AdGuard, hosts, or plain-domain format URLs — one per line. Lists are validated before applying; the last good version is kept for instant rollback if an update is malformed.

◱ Block response

What a blocked domain returns. Block pages show the reason but only work cleanly over HTTP, or with a trusted internal CA for HTTPS.

⌂ Local records

Authoritative A / AAAA / TXT / SRV / CNAME records. Put your apt-cacher-ng _apt_proxy._tcp SRV and Apple content-caching TXT records here.

⇢ Internal zones & forwarders

Internal TLDs (one per line) never leak to public upstreams. Conditional forwarders route a zone to your AD / internal DNS — e.g. stellarad.internal → your domain controller.
Names like speedtest.internal with no forwarder or local record go here — normally OPNsense/pfSense, which is authoritative for them. Never sent to public upstreams.

⌸ Active Directory domain

Point an AD domain at your domain controller in one step. This forwards the whole zone (including _msdcs and all SRV records AD clients need), marks the TLD internal so it never leaks, and sends private reverse (PTR) lookups to the DC — AD-joined machines depend on those.

⚒ Record generators

Build the tricky discovery records correctly. Generated records are appended to your local records above — review, then save there.
